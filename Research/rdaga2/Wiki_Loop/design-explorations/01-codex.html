Quantum &
Mechanics

Category — Physics Origin — 1900–1925 Connexions — 50 Sections — XII
⁂

Quantum mechanics is the fundamental physical theory that describes the behaviour of nature at atomic and subatomic scales. It departs from classical mechanics in positing that energy, momentum and other quantities are often restricted to discrete values, and that objects possess the character of both particles and waves. Instruments, howsoever precise, encounter a limit beyond which measurement itself disturbs the measured. From this peculiar commerce of observer and observed there arose, in the early twentieth century, a new science — strange, exact, and indispensable to all that followed.
