Neural
network

CAT · Machine learning ORIGIN · 1943 REFS · 84 DEPTH · 03

A neural network is a computational model inspired by the structure and function of biological neural networks in the brain. It consists of interconnected nodes (neurons) organised in layers that transform inputs into outputs through weighted connections.

Modern deep networks learn by backpropagating error through these weights. They now underpin most advances in machine perception, language, and generation — from convolutional vision models to the transformer.
